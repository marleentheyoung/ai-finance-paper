# General Referee Report — Narrative & Style
Date: 2026-03-12
Mode: narrative
Round: 3
Sections reviewed: introduction.tex, model.tex, channel1.tex, channel2.tex, channel3.tex, amplification.tex, extensions.tex, conclusion.tex, literature.tex, empirics.tex, appendix.tex, main.tex (abstract)

## What Works

The paper's prose is substantially above-average for a theory manuscript at this stage. The introduction accomplishes its four tasks (question, answer, mechanism, competitors) within roughly two pages, which is efficient. The economic interpretation after each major equation is consistently present and usually sharp -- the "wisdom of AI" versus "herding of AI" framing in Channel 1 is a good example of translating a comparative static into language a non-specialist can grasp. Transition paragraphs between sections are well-constructed; the reader always knows why the next section follows from the current one.

## Summary

The writing is disciplined but not yet tight enough for a top-3 journal. The main problems are: (1) a persistent pattern of restating results that have already been stated, creating redundancy across the introduction, channel sections, amplification section, and conclusion; (2) several passages where technical shorthand substitutes for genuine economic explanation; and (3) the abstract is too long and structurally front-loaded on mechanism rather than result. None of these are blocking, but collectively they add 3-5 pages of avoidable weight.

## Checklist Assessment

### 1. Jargon substituting for explanation
Flagged in G-08 (Katz-Shapiro/Farrell-Saloner mapping) and G-09 (equilibrium concept catalogue). The paper is generally accessible to a broad finance audience, but the extensions section and equilibrium concepts subsection assume subfield-specific knowledge without translation.

### 2. Equation-prose balance
Generally good. The main exception is the model section's opening (G-16), where three displayed equations appear before substantive interpretation. The channel sections and amplification section maintain appropriate equation-prose alternation.

### 3. Bloated passages
Flagged in G-01 (introduction channel descriptions), G-02 (amplification opening recap), G-04 (numerical example redundancy in Channel 1), G-05 (post-proposition redundancy in Channel 2). The paper would lose approximately 3-4 pages if these passages were compressed without loss of content.

### 4. Redundancy across sections
This is the paper's primary narrative weakness. The three-channel summary appears in the introduction, the model section's closing paragraph, the amplification section's opening, and the conclusion. The "safety illusion" concept is described in the introduction, stated formally in the amplification section, and restated in the conclusion. The "no single channel suffices" argument appears at the end of each channel section and again at the start of the amplification section. Flagged in G-01 and G-02.

### 5. Evasive or empty language
Flagged in G-10 (triple scope disclaimer in empirics), G-12 ("still emerging"), G-13 ("the paper's centrepiece"), G-18 (repeated "consistent with"). The paper is generally honest about scope, but the repetition of disclaimers reads as defensive. The self-narrating phrases ("the key insight," "the central result," "the paper's centrepiece") appear roughly five times across the manuscript; each one consumes space without adding content.

### 6. Introduction effectiveness
(a) Question stated in first paragraph: Yes, clearly, in paragraph 2. (b) Answer by end of page 1: Yes, the compound threshold result and safety illusion are stated by paragraph 4-5. (c) Mechanism in non-technical terms: Partially. The channel descriptions are accessible but the amplification loop description (paragraph 4) shifts quickly to formal language ("composite operator mapping the state vector... through three equilibrium mappings"). A finance professor outside this subfield would lose the thread at "Brouwer's fixed-point theorem." (d) Closest competitors named with precise differentiation: Yes, Danielsson-Uthemann and Yang are clearly differentiated. The introduction is effective but could be tighter (G-01) and the mechanism description could remain non-technical for one more sentence before introducing formalism.

### 7. Conclusion quality
The conclusion restates the contribution without overclaiming, which is good. The future work suggestions are specific and credible (dynamic rho, architecture heterogeneity, multi-market extension). The limitations section is over-enumerated (G-07): six items dilute the signal. The final sentence ("Whether the financial system crosses rho* is a question of regulatory design") is a strong closer. The conclusion would benefit from being 20% shorter, achieved by pruning the limitations list and tightening the future-work discussion.

### 8. Abstract
The abstract is approximately 130 words, within range. However, it is structurally misordered (G-03): it leads with setup and mechanism rather than result. The three-channel enumeration ("coordination failure among depositors, collapse of information diversity in prices, and correlated withdrawal by market makers") consumes roughly 20 words that would be better used to state the magnitude or nature of the compound threshold result. The abstract does contain the question, method, main result, and implication -- but the result is buried in the third sentence rather than appearing in the first or second. No extraneous caveats or literature context appears, which is good.

## Recommendation
MINOR REVISION

The prose quality is high relative to the standard theory manuscript at this stage. The blocking issues (G-01, G-02) are structural redundancy problems that can be fixed by cutting repeated material, not by rewriting. The Priority 2 issues are genuine but addressable within a single revision cycle. No logical gaps were identified from a narrative perspective; the communication failures are matters of economy and ordering rather than substance. A disciplined editing pass targeting the flagged redundancies would bring this manuscript to the concision standard expected at a top-3 journal.
