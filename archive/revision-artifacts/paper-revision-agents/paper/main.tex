\begin{abstract}
When financial institutions rely on the same AI models, their private signals become correlated, and three fragility channels activate simultaneously: coordination failure among depositors, collapse of information diversity in prices, and correlated withdrawal by market makers. We parameterise AI signal homogeneity by a single cross-sectional correlation $\rho$ and characterise equilibrium fragility thresholds in each channel within a unified framework. The central result is that the integrated system's compound fragility threshold $\rho^*$ lies strictly below every individual channel threshold, creating a safety illusion in which each channel appears stable while the system is already fragile. Cross-channel amplification arises because correlated liquidity withdrawal raises effective signal correlation, which raises crisis probability, which destroys the incentive to acquire private information, completing a positive feedback loop. A diversity mandate bounding institutional AI model similarity below $\rho^*$ corrects the resulting prisoner's dilemma and restores systemic stability.
\end{abstract}
