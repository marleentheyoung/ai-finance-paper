# General Referee Report — Narrative & Style
Date: 2026-03-12
Mode: narrative
Sections reviewed: introduction.tex, model.tex, channel1.tex, channel2.tex, channel3.tex, amplification.tex, extensions.tex, conclusion.tex, literature.tex, context/model_equations.md, context/research_plan_final.md, context/threat_map_final.md

## What Works

The paper's rhetorical architecture is unusually disciplined for a theory manuscript of this scope. Each channel section ends with a transition paragraph that names the question the next section answers, creating forward momentum that keeps the reader oriented across a multi-channel argument. The introduction accomplishes the rare feat of stating the main result (the strict inequality in equation 1) on page 1 in non-technical terms, and the "wisdom of AI" versus "herding of AI" framing is an effective mnemonic that gives the reader an economic handle on the non-monotonicity result. The notation table in Section 2 is a practical courtesy that more theory papers should adopt.

## Summary

The prose is generally effective but suffers from three systemic problems: (1) substantial redundancy between the introduction and the body sections, where the same economic logic is stated in full three or more times; (2) a pattern of over-narrating results that the equations already convey, inflating section length without adding economic content; and (3) an imbalance in the extensions section, where the prisoner's dilemma setup receives careful treatment but the discussion of dynamic extensions is vague enough to be uninformative. The paper would gain force by losing approximately 15--20% of its word count, concentrated in the redundant restatements of channel mechanisms and the interpretive passages that repeat what the preceding equation already says.

## Mode-Specific Assessment: Communication Effectiveness

### Equation-Prose Balance

The paper generally maintains good equation-prose balance. Displayed equations are almost always followed by interpretive prose. Two exceptions: (a) In the amplification section, equations (8)--(10) defining the composite operator T appear in an aligned block with minimal interstitial prose; a one-sentence interpretation between each mapping would help the reader parse the composition. (b) In the Jacobian analysis, the Jacobian matrix (equation 12) and eigenvalue (equation 13) appear in quick succession with interpretation deferred to the following paragraph. Placing the interpretation immediately after the Jacobian, before the eigenvalue, would better serve sequential comprehension.

### Introduction Effectiveness

The introduction accomplishes tasks (a) through (d) from the checklist, but with significant bloat:
- (a) Question stated in paragraph 1: Yes, clearly.
- (b) Answer by end of page 1: Yes, the main inequality is stated.
- (c) Non-technical mechanism explanation: Partially. The "wisdom of AI" / "herding of AI" framing is accessible, but the five-contribution catalogue that follows is dense with proposition references that a finance professor outside this subfield cannot evaluate.
- (d) Closest competitors and differentiation: Yes, stated precisely, but at excessive length (see G-12).

The introduction's primary failing is length: it attempts to be a compressed version of the entire paper rather than a guide to it. A reader who skips the introduction and reads only the model and results sections would miss nothing of substance, which means the introduction is not earning its page count.

### Conclusion Quality

The conclusion restates the contribution without overclaiming -- a credit. The limitations subsection is specific and honest (static model, exogenous rho in main model, motivating rather than causal empirics, absence of network topology). The future work subsection is weaker: its three directions are generic rather than specific (see G-18). It does not name a concrete testable prediction that the empirical literature could take to data, which is a missed opportunity given that the model generates several (e.g., the non-monotonicity of the crisis threshold, the FPE-RPE divergence, the N_eff formula as a function of observable AI model overlap).

### Abstract Assessment

No abstract was present in the reviewed files. If the abstract exists elsewhere, it should be reviewed against the 100--150 word target containing question, method, main result, and implication. If the abstract does not yet exist, this is a gap that should be addressed before submission.

### Evasive or Empty Language

The manuscript is relatively clean of filler phrases. A few instances to address:
- "it is important to note that" does not appear.
- "The economic intuition is that" appears in channel1.tex. This phrase is a crutch; the sentence following it should be the first sentence of the paragraph, and the phrase deleted.
- "The key insight is that" does not appear, but "The key question is" appears in channel3.tex and "The key factor" in channel2.tex. These are borderline but acceptable as structural markers.
- Excessive hedging is absent. The paper makes its claims directly, which is a strength.

### Redundancy Across Sections: Summary Count

The following ideas are stated more than twice in the paper:

1. **The amplification loop mechanism** (liquidity withdrawal raises effective correlation, which raises crisis threshold, which destroys private information incentive): stated in the introduction, the amplification section opener, the Jacobian paragraph, and the conclusion. Four times. Needed in the introduction and the formal section; the other two should be cut.

2. **The Danielsson-Uthemann differentiation** (rho vs. mu, intensive vs. extensive margin): stated in the introduction, the model section, channel1.tex, the literature review, and the research plan context. Five mentions in the manuscript proper. Once in the introduction and once in the literature review suffices.

3. **The numerical example** (N=100 market makers, N_eff approximately 9.2): stated in the introduction and in channel3.tex. Twice is acceptable if the versions are identical; they should appear in one place only.

4. **The safety illusion** (the interval where all channels look safe but the system is fragile): stated in the introduction, the amplification section, the corollary, and the conclusion. Four times. The introduction and the corollary are necessary; the other restatements are redundant.

5. **Non-monotonicity of the crisis threshold** with the "wisdom/herding" framing: stated in the introduction, channel1.tex (proof sketch, interpretation paragraph, and "wisdom/herding" paragraph), and the conclusion. Five times. Twice is sufficient.

## Recommendation

MAJOR REVISION

The paper communicates its core economics effectively, and the rhetorical architecture (forward-looking transitions, consistent rho-framing, clear differentiation from competitors) is sound. However, the manuscript is substantially over-written. The same ideas are stated three to five times across sections, the introduction attempts to compress the entire paper into three pages rather than guiding the reader into the argument, and several interpretive passages restate what the preceding equation or proposition already conveys. None of these problems affect the substance of the contribution; all are fixable in a single revision pass focused exclusively on compression. The target should be a 15--20% reduction in word count, achieved primarily by (1) cutting the five-contribution catalogue from the introduction, (2) eliminating backward-looking summary paragraphs at the end of each channel section, (3) removing the third and fourth restatements of the amplification loop mechanism, and (4) consolidating the Danielsson-Uthemann comparison to two locations.
