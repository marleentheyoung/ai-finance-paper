## What Works

The paper has a genuinely good idea at its core: parameterising AI-driven fragility through a single cross-sectional correlation primitive and showing that three channels interact to produce a compound threshold below any individual one. The "safety illusion" result is the kind of insight that changes how a reader thinks about macroprudential stress testing, which is the hallmark of a publishable theory paper. The writing is substantially cleaner than a typical first submission -- the introduction states the question in paragraph one, delivers the answer by page one, names competitors, and maps the paper. The three-channel architecture is well motivated and each channel connects to an established foundational literature with precise differentiation.

## Summary

This is an ambitious and well-structured theory paper that delivers a genuine contribution in the amplification loop and bifurcation result. The main reservations concern (a) the ad hoc nature of the cross-channel linking functions (especially Assumption A2), which do much of the work but receive insufficient justification; (b) the gap between the Brouwer existence argument and the paper's narrative, which implicitly treats the fixed point as unique and stable throughout; and (c) the empirical section, which is a placeholder that weakens rather than strengthens the paper. With targeted revisions on these three fronts, the paper is within reach of a top journal.

## Checklist Responses

### 1. What the paper does
The paper formalises how cross-sectional correlation in AI-generated financial signals ($\rho$) creates systemic fragility through three interacting channels -- coordination failure (Goldstein-Pauzner bank runs with Hellwig multiplicity restoration), information acquisition collapse (Grossman-Stiglitz with correlated AI signals crowding out private research), and correlated liquidity withdrawal (effective number of independent market makers collapsing). The central result is that the compound fragility threshold $\rho^*$ of the integrated three-channel system is strictly below each individual channel threshold, creating a "safety illusion" interval where single-channel stress tests are reassuring but the system is already fragile.

### 2. Closest competitors
**(a) Danielsson and Uthemann (2025, JFS):** Derive a crisis threshold $\theta^*(\mu)$ in a global games bank-run model where $\mu$ is the fraction of AI agents. Their primitive is the extensive margin (how many AI agents) vs. this paper's intensive margin (how correlated are AI outputs). Their threshold is linear in $\mu$; this paper's is non-monotone in $\rho$. They model one channel; this paper models three and their interaction. The differentiation is genuine and well-articulated.

**(b) Dugast and Foucault (2018, JFE):** Model how cheap data crowds out costly precise signals in a temporal speed-precision tradeoff. This paper's Channel 2 operates in the cross-sectional dimension (signal correlation across agents, not speed-precision for a single agent). The differentiation is clear but the paper could be more precise about what specifically the cross-sectional dimension adds that the temporal dimension misses.

**(c) Danielsson, Macrae, and Uthemann (2022, JBF):** Provide the most comprehensive qualitative argument for multi-channel AI systemic risk. This paper formalises their qualitative channels into a tractable model with a compound threshold. The contribution relative to this predecessor is formalisation, not conceptual novelty.

### 3. Big-picture coherence
The paper tells a unified story. The three channels build on each other and the amplification loop provides a clear structural reason why they must be analysed jointly. No channel could be removed without weakening the bifurcation result. The weakest link in the architecture is the cross-fundamental connection (Channel 1 uses $\theta$; Channels 2-3 use $V$), which is defended in one sentence rather than derived. But the overall narrative arc -- individual channels create fragility, interaction amplifies it, competitive adoption drives $\rho$ above the compound threshold, diversity mandate resolves the externality -- is coherent and well-paced.

### 4. Logical flow
The paper reads sequentially without major breaks. Two transitions are imperfect: (a) the move from Channel 1 ($\theta$ fundamental) to Channel 2 ($V$ fundamental) requires the reader to accept a change of setting that is not fully motivated until Section 7; (b) the move from the amplification section to the extensions section is abrupt -- the paper goes from proving a bifurcation theorem to a game-theoretic adoption model without a bridge explaining why endogenising $\rho$ is necessary now rather than being a separate paper. The transition paragraphs at the end of each channel section ("Channel X alone does not explain Y; Channel X+1 shows...") are effective.

### 5. Length justification
The paper earns most of its page count. The literature review (Section 2) is the most compressible section -- it could lose 30-40% without losing engagement or positioning. The literature review repeats differentiation points already made in the introduction. The empirical section either needs to be substantive or removed; in its current form it adds pages without adding evidence. The appendix is lean and well-targeted.

### 7. Main result assessment
The bifurcation result ($\rho^* < \min_i \rho_i^*$) is genuinely interesting. It formalises a intuition that regulators and practitioners have expressed informally -- that looking at risk channels separately misses compound effects -- and gives it a precise mathematical characterisation. The safety illusion corollary is the kind of result that would be cited in policy discussions. The non-monotonicity of the crisis threshold is a clean, distinctive prediction that separates this model from Danielsson-Uthemann. The $N_{\text{eff}}$ formula, while algebraically simple, provides a useful conceptual tool.

The results are not mechanical consequences of the setup -- the bifurcation requires the divergence of $h$ near the saddle-node, which is an economic result about how the coordination game becomes fragile near the multiplicity boundary. However, the quantitative strength of the amplification (how much below $\min_i \rho_i^*$ does $\rho^*$ sit?) depends heavily on the functional forms of $g_1$, $g_2$, $g_3$, which are partially assumed rather than derived.

### 8. What works
The paper's main strength is the clarity of its organising framework: one primitive ($\rho$), three channels, one compound threshold. This structure makes the contribution easy to state and easy to remember. The introduction is effective -- a reader knows what the paper does, why it matters, and how it differs from competitors within the first two pages. The engagement with the literature is substantive rather than perfunctory; the differentiation from Danielsson-Uthemann is precise and convincing.

### 9. Overall verdict

**MAJOR REVISION**

The core idea is strong and the paper is well-written. The bifurcation result and safety illusion are genuine contributions that clear the novelty bar for a top finance journal. Three issues require substantive revision: (1) the cross-channel linking function $g_1$ needs a microfoundation or at minimum a robustness analysis demonstrating the bifurcation result survives alternative functional forms; (2) the uniqueness question for the fixed point needs to be resolved or honestly addressed, since the policy recommendations (diversity mandate calibrated to $\rho^*$) require knowing which equilibrium the economy occupies; (3) the empirical section must either present actual results that can be evaluated or be removed in favour of an expanded testable-predictions discussion. None of these issues threatens the core contribution, but each is substantive enough to require work beyond copy-editing.

## Recommendation
**MAJOR REVISION.** The paper has a publishable core: the three-channel amplification loop and bifurcation result represent a genuine advance in understanding AI-driven systemic risk. The issues identified above -- principally the undefended functional form of $g_1$, the unresolved global uniqueness question, and the incomplete empirical section -- are fixable within a revision cycle. The authors should resist the temptation to add more channels or extensions and instead invest revision effort in tightening the foundations of the amplification result.
