## Referee Simulation

**Anticipated major concern 1: Channel 1 differentiation from Danielsson-Uthemann (2025).** A JF/RFS referee familiar with the 2025 JFS paper will note the overlap in the problem domain (AI + global games + coordination failure). The plan now provides a three-part differentiator (rho vs. mu, uniqueness/multiplicity boundary, three-channel integration). This is defensible at a seminar level: the mu parameterisation captures AI speed advantage (extensive margin) while the rho parameterisation captures information homogeneity (intensive margin). The referee will likely accept this as a distinct contribution, especially given that the theta*(rho) derivation is promised as an analytical characterisation of the boundary between uniqueness and multiplicity, which Danielsson-Uthemann do not provide. The referee may request a direct comparison showing that the two models produce qualitatively different predictions (e.g., a case where high mu with low rho is safe but low mu with high rho is dangerous). This is addressable in the paper.

**Anticipated major concern 2: Fixed-point tractability and uniqueness.** The Brouwer argument establishes existence but not uniqueness. A referee will ask whether the multiple fixed-point case is economically meaningful or whether it complicates the comparative statics. The bifurcation result (rho* < min(rho_i*)) is stated for the loss of the "stable interior fixed-point," but without uniqueness, there may be multiple interior fixed-points with different stability properties. The plan honestly defers uniqueness to the Theory Builder. This is acceptable at the plan stage; the Theory Builder must either deliver a contraction mapping argument or show that multiplicity is itself an interesting result (additional equilibrium fragility). A referee will not reject the paper for this but may request clarification.

**Anticipated major concern 3: Empirical proxy credibility.** The ChatGPT release as an instrument for institutional AI adoption remains the weakest element. A referee will argue that ChatGPT is a consumer product and that institutional adoption of AI for trading/risk predates November 2022. The 13F portfolio correlation proxy for rho requires a formal mapping. This concern is mitigated by framing the empirical section as "motivating evidence" rather than causal identification, but the referee may still find it unconvincing. This is the area most likely to generate an R&R request rather than outright rejection.

**Likely recommendation if submitted now:** Revise and Resubmit -- the theoretical framework is strong and novel, the literature positioning is now adequate, and the amplification loop is a genuine contribution. The empirical section needs strengthening but is not fatal given the scope framing. A referee would likely request: (1) formal comparison with Danielsson-Uthemann (2025) predictions, (2) discussion of fixed-point uniqueness/multiplicity, (3) stronger empirical proxy justification.

## Critical Risks

Risk 1: **Channel 1 formal derivation must deliver on the differentiator.** The three-part differentiator from Danielsson-Uthemann (2025) is stated in the plan but not yet proved. If the Theory Builder derives theta*(rho) and it turns out to be structurally isomorphic to theta*(mu) (e.g., just a relabelling), the novelty claim collapses. The Theory Builder must show that the rho parameterisation produces qualitatively different equilibrium dynamics -- specifically, the non-monotonicity and the uniqueness/multiplicity boundary transition that the mu model does not generate. - Severity: Medium (the plan is correct that the mechanisms are economically distinct, but the formal proof is required)

Risk 2: **Fixed-point uniqueness.** The Brouwer argument guarantees existence but not uniqueness. If multiple interior fixed-points exist, the bifurcation result must be restated carefully (which fixed-point bifurcates?). This could complicate the clean "safety illusion" narrative. - Severity: Medium (addressable by the Theory Builder in Phase 4 through contraction mapping or stability analysis)

Risk 3: **Empirical section remains the weakest link.** Unchanged from Iteration 1. The ChatGPT proxy and the 13F-to-rho mapping are informal. However, given the scope constraint (motivating, not causal), this is a Medium rather than High risk. - Severity: Medium
