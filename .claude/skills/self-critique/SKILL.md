# Self-Critique Skill

## Purpose

Apply structured self-critique to a draft, adopting the standards of a demanding journal reviewer to identify problems the author should fix.

Focus on:

- novelty
- identification
- model assumptions
- literature overlap

---

# Review Structure

## Summary

Brief description of the paper and its current state.

---

## Main Contributions

Evaluate novelty and importance.

---

## Major Concerns

Examples:

* unclear equilibrium definition
* unrealistic assumptions
* insufficient differentiation from literature

---

## Minor Comments

Formatting suggestions.

Clarity improvements.

---

## Assessment

Possible assessments:

- Not ready — fundamental issues remain
- Needs significant improvement — core idea sound but execution flawed
- Needs improvement — good draft with identifiable gaps
- Nearly ready — minor issues only
- Ready — draft meets quality standards
