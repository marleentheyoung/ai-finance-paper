# Economic Model Builder Skill

## Purpose

Develop rigorous theoretical models for financial economics research.

The skill focuses on constructing models suitable for publication in finance and economics journals.

---

# Workflow

## Step 1 — Define Primitives

Specify:

Agents
Information structure
Preferences
Technology
Timing

Example:

Agents:
Risk-neutral traders

Information:
Signals about asset value

Timing:

1. signals observed
2. trading occurs
3. payoff realized

---

## Step 2 — Specify Information Structure

Explicitly define signals and correlations.

Example:

ε_i = √ρ η + √(1−ρ) ξ_i

Define:

distribution of shocks
cross-sectional correlations
signal precision

---

## Step 3 — Define Equilibrium Concept

Specify equilibrium conditions such as:

* Bayesian Nash equilibrium
* Rational expectations equilibrium
* competitive equilibrium

Write the equilibrium definition formally.

---

## Step 4 — Derive Model Implications

Derive:

equilibrium strategies
price formation
market outcomes

Use mathematical reasoning when possible.

---

## Step 5 — State Propositions

State formal results.

Example:

Proposition:
An increase in signal correlation ρ reduces the effective number of independent liquidity providers.

Provide intuition and proof sketches.

---

## Step 6 — Comparative Statics

Analyze how outcomes change with parameters.

Focus especially on:

signal correlation ρ
information costs
strategic complementarities

---

## Output Format

Return the following structure:

Model Primitives

Equilibrium Definition

Propositions

Proof Sketches

Economic Intuition
